Professional Summary

PhD researcher in Computer Science with a previous PhD in Mathematics (Harmonic Analysis). My work sits at the interface of theoretical computer science and applied data problems, centered on computational geometry and shape analysis — rotation- and reparameterization-invariant representations of curves and 2D objects, with the algorithmic machinery for shape recognition, retrieval, and large-scale comparison. Adjacent interests run through machine learning, graph algorithms, spectral methods, and the mathematics that underpins them.

Research & Projects

  • Rotation-invariant shape representations: Fixed-length vectorized descriptors for 2D closed curves designed to be invariant to rotation and reparameterization, supporting shape recognition, retrieval, and large-scale comparison.
  • Analysis of star-shaped and non-star-shaped objects: An interdisciplinary method bridging mathematical structure with computational tasks such as boundary representation and 2D object classification.
  • Geometric origami modeling: Python and Rhino-Grasshopper studies of curved-fold behavior, combining mathematical and algorithmic insight.
  • Discrete curve geometry: Curvature, torsion, and shape properties of sampled curves for use in graphics and CAD.
